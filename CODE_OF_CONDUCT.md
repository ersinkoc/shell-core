# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the @oxog/shell-core community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

### ‚úÖ Positive Behaviors
- **Being respectful** - Treating all community members with courtesy and respect
- **Being inclusive** - Welcoming newcomers and helping them feel valued
- **Being collaborative** - Working together constructively to solve problems
- **Being constructive** - Offering helpful feedback and suggestions
- **Being patient** - Understanding that everyone has different skill levels and backgrounds
- **Being professional** - Maintaining appropriate behavior in all interactions
- **Focusing on learning** - Approaching discussions with a growth mindset
- **Giving credit** - Acknowledging the contributions of others
- **Being empathetic** - Considering different perspectives and experiences

### ‚ùå Unacceptable Behaviors
- **Harassment or discrimination** of any kind
- **Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments** toward individuals or groups
- **Personal or political attacks** in project spaces  
- **Publishing others' private information** without consent
- **Sexual language or imagery** and unwelcome sexual attention
- **Spam or off-topic content** that disrupts productive discussions
- **Deliberate intimidation** or aggressive behavior
- **Doxxing or threats** of any kind
- **Other conduct** which could reasonably be considered inappropriate

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include:

- **Using an official project email address**
- **Posting via official social media accounts**  
- **Acting as an appointed representative** at events
- **Contributing to the project** through code, documentation, or discussions
- **Participating in community forums** or chat channels

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. **GitHub Issues**: For public discussions that violate the code of conduct
2. **Direct Contact**: Email the project maintainer at the email associated with their GitHub account
3. **GitHub's Built-in Reporting**: Use GitHub's reporting features for serious violations

### What to Include

When reporting, please include:

- **Your contact information** (if you wish to be contacted)
- **Names of any individuals involved** (including witnesses)
- **Description of the incident** with as much detail as possible
- **Additional context** that may be helpful

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion. We will respect confidentiality requests for the purpose of protecting victims of abuse.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may result in:

### Warning System

1. **First Violation**: Private written warning with explanation
2. **Second Violation**: Temporary restriction from community participation  
3. **Third Violation**: Permanent ban from all community spaces

### Immediate Actions

For severe violations, maintainers may take immediate action including:

- **Temporary or permanent bans** from community spaces
- **Removal of content** that violates the code of conduct
- **Reporting to relevant authorities** when required by law

## Appeals Process

If you believe you have been unfairly sanctioned, you may appeal by:

1. **Contacting project maintainers** with your appeal
2. **Providing additional context** or clarification
3. **Requesting review** of the decision

Appeals will be reviewed fairly and promptly.

## Community Guidelines

### For Contributors

- **Read the documentation** before asking questions
- **Search existing issues** before creating new ones
- **Be specific and clear** in bug reports and feature requests
- **Test your contributions** thoroughly before submitting
- **Follow the project's style guides** and conventions
- **Be patient with review process** and responsive to feedback

### For Maintainers

- **Respond promptly** to community contributions
- **Provide clear feedback** and guidance
- **Be welcoming** to new contributors
- **Maintain consistent standards** for all contributors
- **Lead by example** in following the code of conduct

### For Users

- **Report bugs constructively** with helpful details
- **Show appreciation** for the work of contributors
- **Help other users** when you can
- **Respect the time and effort** of volunteers
